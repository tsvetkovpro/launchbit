How Google Trends Can Improve Your SaaS Blog Content

  • Published by Lee Constantine -- Get updates of new posts here
  • 1 Comment

It should be no surprise that a year-end study  by the Content Marketing Institute and Marketing Profs revealed that 73 percent of B2B marketers produced more content in 2013 than the previous year – not only that, but more than half plan to increase content budget for this year. Content marketing is on the rise and you’ll do right to conform to this social movement.

Image credit: Tapinfluence.com

William Steward, Inbound marketing pro at Inconsive, says “b2b companies that blog produce 67% more leads than companies that don’t.”

But, it takes a lot of time and effort trying to come up with interesting stuff that your prospects and customers would actually want to read. This is why you must make sure you pick relevant topics that are continually growing in popularity.

Enter Google Trends. 81% of B2B buyers start with a web search, and Google Trends does a great job of showing how often a particular search-term is entered relative to the total search-volume across various regions of the world. It paints a pretty good picture of what kinds of content people are interested in.

Andrew Chen, Writer and Entrepreneur, describes Google Trends “as a great tool and a fantastic way to see if the pull demand is increasing or decreasing.”

As a SaaS marketer, you should be familiar with growing trends so you can be sure to include them in your writing. Not only that, but you should also be familiar with fading trends so you’ll know better than to spend any significant amount of time creating your blog content around those topics.

Let’s take a look at some keywords relevant to the b2b SaaS industry and determine if they are worth spending any amount of time on.

“Content Marketing,” as a term, is an obvious first example. Clearly, it’s growing in popularity and importance as more and more b2b marketers are writing about it. Interest in it has been pretty solid even well before 2007 , but in the past couple years (blue line) it has really increased its presence in Google’s indexed content. Looking only in the past 60 days (red line), it has been steadily increasing with the occasional spike in searches.

 

“Audience Targeting” really skyrocketed in late 2007 and any company that was writing about the topic was relevant in the minds of their prospects, customers, and anyone in their respective industry. It has maintained its relevance since late 2007 (blue line) and is still a worthy topic to wrap your blog content around. In the past 60 days (red line) it has shown some real growth as more and more people are still plugging the term into their Google browser.

 

“Landing Pages” has always been relevant to companies who want to capture lead information. It appears that landing pages are more important than ever and show no sign of fading, both in the long term and the short term.

“Lead Generation” was once the holy grail of online business. People wanted any and every piece of information they could find about how to improve their own efforts. This one is tricky, though. Searches for this term have held steady over the past 5 or 6 years (blue line), but it appears to be a fading search term. If you look short term, however, in the past 60 days (red line) it has a significant increase in activity.

“Growth Hacking” is on the up. Rich Roll’s latest writing spree on the hippest new blogging medium, @Medium, speaks on the rise of this ‘hacking’ term and what the trending topic means. “Go ahead and Google any deprivation of the term [hack] and see what comes up. It’s amazing, actually. Consistent with our shrinking attention span and demand for immediate gratification, the hack trend is emblematic of our obsessive modern imperative for immediacy.” Enough said.

 

“Outbound Marketing” surprisingly is still thriving in the Google search box. You would think that with the rise of b2b inbound marketing, outbound marketing would become less and less relevant – and maybe that’s true as far as actual implementation (maybe not) – either way, people are talking about it so you should be writing about it.

 

“Inbound Marketing” is without a doubt increasing – along with it’s implementation by SaaS companies all over the world. This topic is on the minds of marketers everywhere and the growing trend supports this, both short term and long term.

If you want to know what kinds of content people find interesting, use Google Trends to search keywords most relevant to your customers or your target audience.

A caveat to using such a simple tool is that mobile apps don’t often benefit from search engine traffic the same way that websites do, since none of the content in the apps are being indexed – which means the trends in Google will not display mobile traffic. Thus, remember that this analysis is strongest for content searched on the web, and to think of this as good starting point for developing your blog content and topics.

Jonathan Abrams, Founder of Friendster, says “the sad truth about blogging is that 99% of of them are not worth reading.” Don’t be one of those blogs. Stay relevant.
