3 ways to reach hyper-targeted business audiences using Facebook

  • Published by Elizabeth Yin -- Get updates of new posts here
  • 6 Comments

Facebook ads have gone through massive transitions over the years. In the beginning, the targeting capabilities were very simple: select demographic options and get your campaign in front of people who met those demographics. The problem with this was that you didn’t have the ability to go granular in your campaign. If you wanted to reach small business owners, they put your ad in front of EVERYONE who was even remotely involved with a small business. But what if you just wanted to reach say small business owners who were Constant Contact users? There wasn’t a way to get that granular.

Today, Facebook has an immense number of ad targeting options that most people don’t know about but are really powerful. Here are 3 ways to hyper-targeted business audiences on Facebook.

1) Retarget visitors to your site

This is the most well-known of the three options. You can cookie visitors to your site and then display ads to those people when they browse Facebook. You can set this up with companies like Perfect Audience, AdRoll, and ReTargeter.

2) Create custom audiences

Retargeting is a good way to reach high intent potential customers. But, the limitation to retargeting ads is that your max reach is the total number of people coming to your site. So, how do you get more reach but still hyper-target.

Custom audiences on Facebook is an area that I’m super excited about. You can simply upload a list of email addresses of people you want to target, and your ad will get in front of a subset of those people. Pretty amazing. Here’s how to do this:

A) In the Ads Manager within your account, click on “Audiences”.

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

B) Click on “Create Audiences”.  Then select “Data File Custom Audience”.  Upload your CSV file with your list of email addresses — people you would like to get your ad campaign in front of.

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

So what list of email addresses should you upload? You could upload your list of prospectives — people who signed up for your free trial or perhaps your ebook or white paper. Alternatively, if you don’t have a large list of prospective customers, we at LaunchBit can help you generate a list through our custom audiences program and run those ads for you.  This program is in beta.

3) Create look-alike custom audiences

Lastly, once you have a list of custom audiences, Facebook can help you augment your reach further by getting your campaign in front of other people who are similar to those on your list. Unlike their old targeting options, this feature allows you to decide how closely similar you want this augmented audience to be. Here’s how to set this up:

A) Once you have followed the steps to create a custom audience above, go to the Power Editor and select “Ad Tools”.

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

B) Select your custom audience list and then press “Create Lookalike Audience”.
